Compare the lenses

Trust in science is a complicated, ever-changing construct — which makes it hard to get a complete picture of its current state. Using this tool you can roughly compare the four lenses of trust across multiple countries. Each lens will help focus your attention on a single dimension of trust in science and help you understand the relative importance of that dimension for a country of your choice. If a particular lens is more important than others — you can use that knowledge to mitigate for the identified blocker.

The Lens Of Diffusion

How is the perception of science affected by its alignment with different groups in society?

Examing how South Africa's experiences with the state may be influencing how people view science and scientists.

CONSIDER

1. Is this project being delivered by partners at the country level?

2. Consider the level of general & specific distrust in the country of intervention. Is any mitigation needed?

Countries which show high distrust in members of society (neighbourhood, doctors and nurses, journalists and non profits) also show high distrust in scientists (correlation of 0.74 for scientists in their country and 0.69 for science).

The Lens Of Distance

How far removed might people feel they are from the benefits of science?

Highlighting areas in Kenya, Malawi and South Africa which perceive themselves to be excluded from the positive activities and effects of science.

CONSIDER

1. Is this project targeting a particular community or region?

2. Consider how the evidence you are creating can benefit the most vulnerable and historically disadvantaged groups in the country?

Distrust correlates with feeling excluded. Globally, distrust in science is moderately correlated (0.65 correlation) with saying science does not benefit their country.

The Lens Of Attraction

Understanding the desirability of a world shaped by science and technology for India and Bangladesh.

CONSIDER

1. Is the project based in a country which is going through a significant transformation by science and technology?

2. What is the strength of the agricultural, industrial or service sectors in the country in question?

The greater the experience of the transformation of society by science and technology the less certain the respondents are of the role science and technology can play in creating jobs.
